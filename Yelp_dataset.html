Analysis plan¶

- Yelp business model:¶

  • two-side market: personal users and businesses (primary source of revenue)
  • revenue source: ads/traffic/Yelp reservation

- questions/objective:¶

  • restaurants
    • what could lead to more reviews?
    • what could lead to higher review stars?
    • target restaurants for sales
  • users
    • who are valuable users (how to measure)?
    • compliments vs elite vs number of friends
  • reviews
    • lenth vs usefulness

- assumptions¶

  • the dataset of reviews is a random sample and is good representation of all the reviews
  • OLS assumes simple linear replationships

- next steps¶

  • combine number of reviews and starters into one expression for modeling
  • identify poor-reviewed restaurants by comparing with peers (nearby/similar type)
  • time series analysis (trend, seasonality and noise of engagement)
  • geo vs restaurant type preferrence
  • target audience market for user acquisition (need more info)

# ratio of restaurants having changed stars in the past year
stars_change = pd.DataFrame()
stars_change['stars_2014'] = review[review.year=='2014'].groupby(['business_id'])['review_stars'].mean()
stars_change['reviews_2014'] = review[review.year=='2014'].groupby(['business_id']).size()
stars_change['stars_2013'] = review[review.year=='2013'].groupby(['business_id'])['review_stars'].mean()
stars_change['reviews_2013'] = review[review.year=='2013'].groupby(['business_id']).size()
stars_change['abs_change'] = abs(stars_change.stars_2014-stars_change.stars_2013)
res_filtered = stars_change[(stars_change.reviews_2014>10)&(stars_change.reviews_2013>10)] # filter out restaurants with very few reviews
r = 1.*len(res_filtered[res_filtered.abs_change>=.5])/len(res_filtered)
print 'Based on {} restaurants with at least 10 reviews each year, {:.1%} have a change of at least half a star from 2013 to 2014.'.format(len(res_filtered),r)
